## STORY 1: Mahama nominates three justices — Supreme Court set to grow to 21 — Politics/Governance — CONFIRMED

**Key facts:** On **16 July 2026**, President **John Dramani Mahama** nominated three people to the **Supreme Court** under Article 144 of the Constitution: Court of Appeal Justices **Sophia Bernasko-Essah** and **Amoako Asante** (a former President of the ECOWAS Court of Justice), and legal practitioner **Tony Forson**, a former President of the Ghana Bar Association. If approved, the nominations would take the apex court from **18 to 21 justices**. All three must go through **parliamentary vetting and approval** before they can be sworn in. Attribute the appointments strictly to the President; the process is not yet complete.

## STORY 2: Fuel prices go up from July 16 — Business — CONFIRMED

**Key facts:** Pump prices rose across Ghana from **Thursday 16 July 2026**, the second pricing window of the month. The **National Petroleum Authority (NPA)** raised its indicative price floors: **petrol from GH¢12.79 to GH¢13.28 per litre** and **diesel from GH¢13.54 to GH¢14.35 per litre**, with a small LPG adjustment (about GH¢10.11 to GH¢10.19 per kg). The **Chamber of Oil Marketing Companies (COMAC)** projected petrol rising **3.79%–5.31%** to an average pump price of about **GH¢14.52 per litre**, with diesel around **GH¢16.00 per litre**. COMAC blamed *"rising global petroleum prices and a slight depreciation of the Ghana cedi"* — with the **Strait of Hormuz crisis** pushing Brent crude above **US$80** a barrel. Cedi context: about **GH¢11.55 to the dollar interbank** on 17 July (Trading Economics), roughly GH¢12.30 retail.
