## STORY 1: 24-Hour Economy Signs $5.5bn In Deals — Ghana business & economy — Confirmed

**Verified facts:** The 24-Hour Economy & Accelerated Export Development Secretariat says it has signed US$5.5bn in joint development agreements with co-development partners, inside an US$11.5bn project pipeline. Four recent deals target 160,000+ direct jobs, toward a stated 1.7 million jobs by end-2028. Flagship projects: Buipe solar/battery ($1.45bn, ~1,500MW, ~13,000 jobs); Kambonwule oil palm complex ($250m, ~120,000 jobs). Shared in response to the Minority (Kojo Oppong Nkrumah). **Caution:** these are agreements signed / pipeline value as stated by the government Secretariat — not disbursed investment.
**Sources:** MyJoyOnline — https://www.myjoyonline.com/weve-signed-5-5bn-with-co-development-partners-to-transform-ghanas-economy-1-7m-jobs-to-be-created-24-hour-economy-secretariat/ · Graphic Online — https://www.graphic.com.gh/news/general-news/268-fuelling-stations-33-manufacturers-now-operating-under-24-hour-economy-secretariat.html · Ghana Business News — https://www.ghanabusinessnews.com/2026/07/17/24-hour-economy-secures-5-5b-investment-deals-160000-jobs-coming-secretariat/

### A. Facebook
💼 GHANA'S 24-HOUR ECONOMY SAYS IT HAS SIGNED $5.5 BILLION IN DEALS.

The 24-Hour Economy and Accelerated Export Development Secretariat says it has signed $5.5bn worth of joint development agreements with partners — part of a wider $11.5bn project pipeline.

The Secretariat says four of the recent deals alone target more than 160,000 direct jobs, on the way to a stated goal of 1.7 million jobs by the end of 2028. Flagship projects include a $1.45bn solar and battery plant at Buipe and a $250m oil palm complex at Kambonwule.

One important note: these are agreements signed and a pipeline of planned investment — not money already spent. The figures were shared as the Secretariat answered questions from the Minority.
