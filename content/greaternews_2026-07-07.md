## STORY 2: Ghana settles $700m Eurobond early — Business — CONFIRMED

**Key facts:** The Ministry of Finance fully settled a US$700m Eurobond obligation on July 2, ahead of schedule — US$525.2m principal and US$174.8m interest — paid, per the Ministry, without undue pressure on FX reserves. Total paid to Eurobond holders since January 2025: US$2.1bn under the Debt Exchange Programme terms. Officials say it strengthens investor confidence and shows commitment to debt discipline.

### A. Facebook

GHANA JUST PAID $700 MILLION OF ITS DEBT — EARLY.

The Ministry of Finance has fully settled a US$700 million Eurobond obligation ahead of schedule: $525.2 million in principal and $174.8 million in interest.

That brings Ghana's total payments to Eurobond holders since January 2025 to US$2.1 billion — all under the debt exchange programme.

The Ministry says the payment was made without straining the country's foreign reserves, and analysts say early settlement strengthens investor confidence in the recovery.

(Source: Ministry of Finance | GBC | The Herald)

## STORY 3: Floods hit Accra again — military deployed — Politics/Public safety — CONFIRMED

Floodwater entered homes, displacing families. The Ghana Armed Forces deployed under an emergency operation code-named Operation Boafo, led by the 48 Engineer Regiment under Lt Col Frank Osei Amponsah. This follows the June 29 floods that killed at least 13 people. Somber treatment — no dramatic imagery.
