## STORY 1: National Cleaning Day — Ghana cleans up today — Politics/Civic — CONFIRMED

**Key facts:** President Mahama declared July 10-11 National General Cleaning Days in seven flood-affected regions, themed "Our Actions, Our Future: Cleaning Ghana after the Floods," run by the Post-Flood Mitigation Committee. Security agencies, MMDAs and waste companies started Friday; the general public joins today (Saturday). All government appointees — ministers, MPs, MMDCEs, heads of institutions — were directed to personally lead cleanups in their communities. Focus: desilting choked drains, clearing roads, cleaning markets and lorry parks.

## STORY 3: Congo Ebola outbreak passes 600 deaths — World/Health — CONFIRMED

**Key facts:** Congo's Ebola outbreak has killed 600 people, with 1,759 confirmed cases. Suspected cases have appeared in Tshopo and Haut-Uele provinces — beyond the Ituri epicenter — including in Kisangani. A WHO official says the outbreak is "spreading largely undetected"; four in five new cases have no known link to existing patients, so the true scale may be 2-4× official figures. Africa CDC calls it the continent's fastest-growing Ebola outbreak. It's the rare Bundibugyo strain — no approved vaccine; treatment trials began last week. Health-story rules: officials' words only, no advice of our own.

### A. Facebook
