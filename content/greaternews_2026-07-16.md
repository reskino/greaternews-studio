## STORY 1: Ghana Card photocopying now a criminal offence — Consumer — CONFIRMED

**Key facts:** The NIA announced July 15 that an amendment to the National Identity Register Regulations (L.I. 2111) has been gazetted making biometric verification the ONLY lawful way to verify a Ghana Card holder in a transaction. Photocopying or visually inspecting the card for a transaction is now an offence — extending beyond banking to ALL organisations. Fines: institutions GH¢6,000–24,000; individuals GH¢600–6,000. NIA's stated reason: photocopies fuel identity theft.
