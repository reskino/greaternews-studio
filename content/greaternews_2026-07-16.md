## STORY 1: Photocopying your Ghana Card is now a criminal offence — Governance/Consumer — CONFIRMED

**Key facts:** On Wednesday **15 July 2026**, NIA Executive Secretary/CEO **Wisdom Kwaku Deku** announced that an amendment to the National Identity Register Regulations has been **gazetted**, making **biometric verification the only lawful way** to verify a Ghana Card holder in a transaction. **Photocopying or visually inspecting** the card for a transaction is now an offence, and the rule now extends **beyond banks to all organisations**. Fines on summary conviction: **institutions GH¢6,000–24,000; individuals GH¢600–6,000.** NIA's stated reason: photocopies fuel identity fraud and misuse of personal data.

And the fines are real: **GH¢6,000 to GH¢24,000 for institutions, GH¢600 to GH¢6,000 for individuals.**

Why? The NIA says photocopied cards are feeding identity theft.
