## STORY 1: One killing, 979 evacuations — what is happening to Ghanaians in South Africa? — World news / Diaspora — CONFIRMED

**Own angle:** Other outlets are reporting the killing OR the witness OR the evacuations. Nobody is connecting them. GreaterNews tells the full story: one man's death has become a national response — autopsy, repatriation, a formal protest, an African Union petition, and nearly a thousand Ghanaians brought home.

**Key verified facts:**
- Bashiru Isak, 40, a Ghanaian, was shot dead on Monday, June 30, 2026 in Khayelitsha, Cape Town, during anti-immigrant unrest (GNA, Citi Newsroom, The Herald).
- He was working in a shop with a Zimbabwean colleague when, per the witness account, a group entered and ordered them to leave the country (MyNewsGH, 3News).
- The key eyewitness has gone into hiding fearing for his life; the High Commission is seeking witness protection so he can testify (3News, MyNewsGH).
- High Commissioner Benjamin Anani Quashie disputes the police description of an extortion case, calling the killing xenophobic (MyNewsGH). South African police maintain the extortion line — both positions must always be attributed.
- Ghana's Ministry of Foreign Affairs condemned the killing "in the strongest terms," formally protested to South Africa's DIRCO, lodged a complaint with SAPS, arranged an autopsy, is repatriating the body, and has petitioned the African Union Commission over xenophobic violence (GNA).
- 979 Ghanaians have been evacuated from South Africa in three batches (GNA).
